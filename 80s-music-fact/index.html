80's Music Facts

From "Footloose" to "Top Gun," the 80s produced some of the most memorable movie soundtracks. These songs not only defined films but also became part of the cultural soundtrack of the decade.

In 1981, MTV (Music Television) made its debut, forever changing the way we experience music by bringing visuals to the forefront.

Rolling Stone said that Michael Jackson's Thriller made music videos really cool. The person in charge, John Landis, called Thriller a story about growing up. He thought the characters growing hair and becoming werewolves was like a symbol for going through teenage changes.

Rock legends ruled the airwaves with anthems that are still belted out today. Journey's "Don't Stop Believin'" and Queen's "Another One Bites the Dust" became timeless classics.

The 80s was the era of unforgettable pop tunes. From Wham!'s "Wake Me Up Before You Go-Go" to Katrina and the Waves' "Walking on Sunshine," the decade was a non-stop party.

Big hair, leather, and power ballads dominated the 80s rock scene. Bands like Bon Jovi and Def Leppard ruled the charts, creating an anthemic sound that still echoes in our hearts.
